\subsection{Closed-set segmentation in open-world scenes}
\label{subsec:intro-closed-set}
Writing plan: start from the standard formulation of semantic, instance, and panoptic segmentation, where the label space is fixed during training and evaluation. Emphasize that this closed-set assumption is fragile in open-world scenes because new object categories, long-tail concepts, and user-specific categories are not covered by the training taxonomy. This paragraph should motivate why open-vocabulary segmentation is not only a benchmark variation but also a necessary step toward deployable visual understanding.

\subsection{Foundation models as the driver of open-vocabulary and reasoning segmentation}
\label{subsec:intro-foundation-models}
Writing plan: explain the 2023--2025 transition. CLIP-style image-text alignment enables category names to act as classifiers in open-vocabulary segmentation, as represented by OVSeg and SAN \citep{liang2023ovseg,xu2023san}. SAM reframes segmentation as prompt-conditioned mask generation \citep{kirillov2023sam}. Diffusion features and unified decoders broaden the task from semantic maps to panoptic and multimodal outputs \citep{xu2023odise,zou2023xdecoder}. MLLM-based methods then connect complex language instructions with pixel masks \citep{lai2024lisa,rasheed2024glamm}.
